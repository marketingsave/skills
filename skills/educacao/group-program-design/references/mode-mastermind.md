# Mastermind Mode — Deep Reference

Peer advisory group. No rigid curriculum. Facilitator holds structure, not content.

## Brief — Mode-Specific Inputs

- Ideal member profile (revenue range — within 2-3x of each other, business stage, industry)
- Paid or free
- Facilitated or peer-led (rotating)

## Mastermind Brief Template

```
## Mastermind Brief
**Group:** [Name]
**Purpose:** [Business growth / specific skill / industry]
**Members:** 6-8
**Cadence:** Bi-weekly, 90 minutes
**Duration:** 6 months
**Price:** $300/month
**Facilitation:** Facilitator-led (or rotating peer-led)
```

## Framework — Hot Seat Meeting Format

No linear curriculum. The structure is the meeting format.

```
## Standard Meeting Agenda (90 min)

**Opening Round (10 min)**
Each member: one win, one number, one word for how they're feeling

**Hot Seats (60 min)** — 2-3 members per meeting, 20 min each:
- Member presents challenge (5 min)
- Group clarifying questions (3 min)
- Group advice and ideas (10 min)
- Member states commitment/next step (2 min)

**Accountability Review (10 min)**
Review commitments from last meeting — did each follow through?

**Closing Round (10 min)**
One takeaway + one action item for the next two weeks.
```

## Hot Seat Rules

1. Hot seat member speaks first — no interruptions during 5-min setup
2. Questions before advice — understand before solving
3. Be direct but respectful
4. Offer frameworks, not just opinions ("Have you tried X?" beats "I think you should...")
5. The hot seat member decides what advice to take
6. Confidentiality is absolute

## Member Selection (mastermind-critical)

```
## Ideal Member Profile
- Revenue range: [$X-$Y — members within 2x of each other]
- Business stage: [Early / growth / scaling]
- Industry: [Same / adjacent / mixed — define]
- Commitment: available for all meetings, willing to do prep
- Attitude: gives as much as takes, open to feedback

## Application Questions
1. Current business and revenue?
2. Biggest challenge right now?
3. What do you bring? (expertise, connections, perspective)
4. Can you commit to [cadence] for [duration]?
5. Past mastermind experience — likes/dislikes?
```

## Operations

**Recruitment:**
- Application-based — selection matters more than volume
- Revenue/stage screening is non-negotiable
- Review applications, select, send invitations 2 weeks before kickoff
- Offer a free trial meeting if applications are thin

**Onboarding (mode-specific):** hot seat prep form, accountability tracker shared doc, member directory.

**Pricing:** Monthly subscription model ($200-$500/month standard), 6-month minimum commitment.

**Facilitator posture:** manage time/energy/structure, not content. Do not do all the advising.

## Assessment — Health Metrics + Renewal

Track monthly:
- **Attendance rate** — target 85%+
- **Hot seat utilization** — are all members using their turns?
- **Commitment follow-through rate** — target 70%+
- **Member satisfaction** — anonymous monthly pulse
- **Renewal intent** — ask at month 4

End-of-term: celebrate wins, collect feedback, offer renewal for next 6-month term.

## Mastermind Health Targets

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Attendance | 85%+ meetings |
| Completion | 70%+ commitments |
| Satisfaction (NPS) | 50+ |
| Renewal | 60%+ |

## Anti-Patterns (Mastermind)

- **No hot seat structure** — rambling conversations. Use the prep form and timer.
- **Revenue mismatch** — a $2K/mo member has different problems than a $50K/mo member. Stay within 2-3x.
- **No accountability tracking** — if commitments are not revisited, members stop making them.
- **Facilitator doing all the advising** — the group should advise.
- **No confidentiality agreement** — members will not share real challenges.

## Recovery (Mastermind)

- **Not enough applicants**: lower barrier or offer a free trial meeting.
- **One member dominates**: use the timer; if it continues, have a private conversation about participation norms.
- **Group energy drops after month 2**: introduce a new element — guest expert, off-site, or goal sprint.
- **Member wants to leave early**: allow graceful exit with 30-day notice. A disengaged member drags the group down.
