# Preset: Launch

## Objective
Drive purchase during a time-bound cart-open window. Build desire before the cart opens, create urgency before it closes. Every email must advance the narrative or it does not belong.

## When to Pick This Preset
- Launching a product, course, or service with a defined cart open/close window
- Need pre-launch hype, launch window, AND post-launch follow-up
- Segmented buyer/non-buyer post-launch messaging

**Do NOT use for:** evergreen funnels, ongoing newsletters, transactional emails. For simple launches under 6 emails with no pre-launch phase, use the simple launch variant below.

## Typical Shape

Two variants:

### Simple Launch (6 emails / 7 days)
| # | Day | Name | Purpose |
|---|-----|------|---------|
| 1 | Day 1 | The Announcement | Reveal the offer, generate excitement |
| 2 | Day 2 | The Deep Dive | Explain what's included and who it is for |
| 3 | Day 3 | The Social Proof | Testimonials, results, case studies |
| 4 | Day 5 | The Objection Handler | Address top 1–2 hesitations |
| 5 | Day 6 | The Deadline Warning | Window closing |
| 6 | Day 7 | The Final Call | Last chance, cart closes tonight |

### Full Launch (11–13 emails / 7–14 days)
Four phases + post-launch split:

**Phase A: Pre-Launch (Days -7 to -1)** — seed the problem, share origin story, build anticipation. **Zero buy links.**
- E1 (Day -7): Problem awareness — "The hidden cost of [pain]"
- E2 (Day -4): Story/origin — "Why I built [product]"
- E3 (Day -1): Anticipation — "Something big is coming tomorrow"

**Phase B: Cart Open (Days 0–1)** — full offer reveal, lead with transformation not features. Include early bird bonus if applicable.
- E4 (Day 0): Launch announcement
- E5 (Day 1): Deep dive — transformation and benefits

**Phase C: Mid-Launch (Days 3–5)** — each email a different angle. No repetition.
- E6 (Day 3): Case study / social proof
- E7 (Day 4): Objection handling — "Is this right for you?"
- E8 (Day 5): FAQ roundup

**Phase D: Cart Close (Days 6–7)** — urgency must be real.
- E9 (Day 6): 24-hour warning
- E10 (Day 7): Final hours — last chance
- E11 (Day 7, evening): Cart closed + waitlist

**Post-Launch (Day 8)** — segmented:
- E12 (buyers): Thank you / what's next
- E13 (non-buyers): Nurture back — no guilt

## Required Inputs (beyond universal)

| Input | What to Ask | Default |
|-------|------------|---------|
| **Product/offer** | "What are you launching?" | No default |
| **Price point** | "What's the price? Early bird? Bonuses?" | No default |
| **Launch dates** | "When does cart open and close?" | 7-day launch window |
| **Audience size** | "How many subscribers?" | Under 5,000 |
| **Audience warmth** | "Have they heard about this before?" | Some awareness from content |
| **Bonuses/scarcity** | "Limited bonuses, early bird, enrollment caps?" | None |

## Phase-Specific Rules
- **Pre-launch (A):** No selling. Seed the problem, share story, build anticipation. Zero buy links.
- **Cart open (B):** Lead with transformation, not features. Include early bird bonus expiration if applicable.
- **Mid-launch (C):** Each email tackles one *different* objection or angle. No repetition.
- **Cart close (D):** Urgency must be real — the cart actually closes. Last-24h email = countdown + final benefit restate.
- **Post-launch:** Buyers get celebration + onboarding handoff. Non-buyers get value, never guilt — they may buy next time.

## Segmentation (mark on every email)
- **Audience filter:** full list / openers-only / clicked-sales-page / etc.
- **Exclusion rule:** buyers must be excluded from all emails after their purchase
- **Segment-specific replacements:** E11 splits into E12 (buyers) and E13 (non-buyers)

## Launch Day Checklist
- [ ] All emails scheduled with correct dates and times
- [ ] Sales page live and tested
- [ ] Payment processing tested
- [ ] Buyer tag/automation configured
- [ ] Cart close mechanism ready (redirect or page update)
- [ ] Post-launch emails queued

## Key Metrics
- **Cart-open conversion:** 2–5% of engaged list
- **Revenue per subscriber:** RPS = total revenue / list size receiving sequence
- **Sales distribution:** typically 30% on cart open day, 40–50% on cart close day, rest in the middle
- **Unsubscribe rate during launch:** up to 1–2% is normal

## Preset-Specific Anti-Patterns
- **Launching without pre-launch.** Zero-to-"buy now" fails. Warm the audience first.
- **Same angle every email.** Repeating "here are the features" kills engagement. Vary the angle.
- **Fake urgency.** If the cart does not actually close, do not say it does. Audiences remember.
- **Forgetting to exclude buyers.** Sending "buy now" to existing customers is insulting.
- **No post-launch plan.** Sequence does not end at cart close. Follow up with both segments.

## Recovery
- **No launch dates set:** Recommend 7-day launch window starting 10 days from now. Adjust pre-launch timing accordingly.
- **First-ever launch (no social proof):** Use beta tester feedback, personal results, or "why I created this" narrative in place of case studies.
- **Small list (under 500):** Shorten to 8 emails total. Focus on personal connection over broadcast copy.
- **User wants more than 15 emails:** Warn of fatigue. Max 13 over 2 weeks.
- **No bonuses or scarcity:** Create urgency through genuine deadline (enrollment closes on X), not manufactured scarcity.
