# Review Workflow — Request, Respond, Repeat

Reviews drive prominence (Google's 3rd pillar) and conversion (social proof). Target: consistent velocity + 100% response rate + never fake.

## Platform Priority (where to send reviewers)

1. **Google Business Profile** — highest SEO impact, always primary
2. **Industry-specific** (always #2 if relevant):
   - Restaurants: TripAdvisor, Yelp (US), TheFork (EU)
   - Medical: Healthgrades, Zocdoc, Doctoralia
   - Legal: Avvo, Martindale
   - Home services: Angi, HomeAdvisor
   - Hospitality: Booking.com, Expedia
3. **Facebook** (consumer trust, not ranking)
4. **Yelp** (US only — do NOT solicit, Yelp's policy bans asking)

Never send one customer to multiple platforms simultaneously. Rotate or segment.

## Request Timing

- **Immediately after peak emotion** — service completion, successful delivery, problem solved
- Restaurants: table-side card at bill drop, SMS 2h after booking marked complete
- Medical/dental: SMS 2h post-appointment (not while they're still numb)
- Home services: text from the tech's phone as they pack up
- E-commerce w/ local pickup: email 48h after pickup
- SaaS with local onboarding: 14 days after go-live, only to contacts with NPS ≥ 9

Rule: if you wait 7+ days, conversion to written review drops by roughly half.

## Cadence & Velocity Targets

- Absolute minimum: 2 reviews/month (below this signals dormancy)
- Healthy: 5–10/month
- Aggressive: 15–30/month (requires systematic ask on every transaction)
- Velocity consistency > spikes — 5/month for 12 months beats 60 in month 1 and zero after

Never more than ~10% of total reviews in a single week (spike = Google filter risk).

## Request Templates

### SMS (best conversion, ~20–30%)

> Hi [Name], it was great seeing you today at [Business]. Would you mind sharing a quick review of your visit? It takes under a minute: [shortlink]. Thanks — [First Name of owner/tech]

> Olá [Nome]! Obrigado pela visita à [Clínica]. Se puder deixar uma breve avaliação no Google, ajuda-nos imenso: [shortlink]. Obrigado! — [Nome]

### Email (lower conversion, ~5–10%, use if no SMS consent)

Subject: Quick favor, [Name]?

> Hi [Name],
>
> Thanks for choosing [Business] for your [service] on [date]. If we earned it, a quick Google review would genuinely help a small local business like ours — and it takes about 60 seconds.
>
> [Big button: Leave a Google review]
>
> If anything fell short, please just reply to this email and I'll fix it personally.
>
> — [Owner first name]
> [Business]

### In-Person Card (dentist, restaurant, salon, home service)

Hand-size card with QR code pointing to `g.page/[your-profile]/review` + one line: "Your review in 60 seconds helps our small team enormously. Thank you."

### Shortlink Setup

Use your GBP review shortlink (`g.page/r/XXXXXX/review`) — grab from GBP dashboard. Never use `search.google.com/local/writereview?placeid=...` links in SMS (they break on some Android clients).

## Response Templates

Respond within 48h. 100%. Use the reviewer's name. Reference specifics. Sign with real name.

### Positive (4–5 stars)

> Thank you [Name] — so glad [specific thing they mentioned] worked out. We'll let [team member] know you called them out. See you next time!
> — [Owner first name], [Business]

Avoid: copy-paste "Thanks for the 5 stars!" on every review. Google's NLP spots template responses and they look lazy to prospects reading them.

### Neutral (3 stars)

> [Name], thank you for the honest feedback. You're right that [acknowledge specific issue]. We've [concrete action taken or planned]. I'd love the chance to make it right — [owner email/phone].
> — [Owner first name]

### Negative (1–2 stars)

Rules:
- Never defensive, never argue public facts
- Acknowledge the experience (not necessarily the claim)
- Move resolution OFF the public review
- Sign with real name + direct contact
- Never reveal private information (medical details, transaction amounts, personal history)

Template:

> [Name], I'm sorry your experience didn't meet the standard we work hard to deliver. I'd like to understand what happened and make it right personally — could you email me at [owner@business] or call [direct line]? — [First name], owner

### Fake / Extortion / Policy-Violating Reviews

1. Flag via GBP with specific policy cited (spam, conflict of interest, off-topic, restricted content, illegal, terrorist, sexually explicit, offensive, dangerous, impersonation)
2. If flag ignored in 72h, escalate via GBP support tweet (@GoogleMyBiz) or community forum
3. Document in a log; patterns matter for appeals

## Governance

- Central inbox monitored daily for review notifications (GBP, Yelp, niche)
- SLA dashboard: % responded within 48h, average response time, reviews this week
- Monthly review meeting: themes, root causes, template updates
- Training: every customer-facing employee knows the ask script and the shortlink

## Never Do

- Gate reviews (only send happy customers to Google) — Google policy violation
- Offer discounts, gifts, entries to win — FTC violation (US) + Google policy
- Buy reviews / use review farms — detection = removal + suspension
- Fake reviews from staff / family — IP + device fingerprints catch these
- Respond with threats of legal action — always escalates
- Delete bad reviews on platforms you control — engage instead
