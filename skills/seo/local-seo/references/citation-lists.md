# Citation Sources — Priority Lists by Country and Niche

Build Tier-1 first (universal, high authority). Then country-specific. Then niche. NAP must match GBP byte-for-byte on every entry.

## Tier 1 — Universal (do these everywhere)

1. Google Business Profile (the source of truth)
2. Apple Maps / Apple Business Connect
3. Bing Places for Business
4. Facebook Business Page
5. Yelp
6. Foursquare / Factual (feeds many other data aggregators)
7. LinkedIn Company Page
8. Instagram Business (NAP in bio)
9. YouTube channel About section
10. TripAdvisor (if consumer-facing)

Data aggregators (US-centric but global reach): Neustar/Localeze, Data Axle, Foursquare. Submitting here propagates to 100+ downstream directories.

## Workflow

1. Audit existing citations (Moz Local / BrightLocal / Whitespark scan)
2. Correct inconsistencies BEFORE adding new ones
3. Submit top 10 universal + top 5 country + top 5 niche = ~20 priority citations
4. Log each in a tracker: directory, URL, submission date, login, NAP as submitted, live date
5. Quarterly re-audit — directories silently edit or merge listings

## Cost vs. Manual

- Manual: free but 10–20 min per citation
- Managed (Yext, Moz Local, BrightLocal, Whitespark): $99–$999/year per location, worth it for 10+ locations or when manual cleanup exceeds 50 directories

## What NOT to Do

- Mass-submission services that spray 500+ low-quality directories — hurts more than helps
- Do not vary NAP for "keyword diversity" — byte-for-byte only
- Do not use tracking numbers as the citation phone — always primary local number
