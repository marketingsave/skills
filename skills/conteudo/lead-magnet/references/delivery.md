# Delivery — Email Sequence & Output Structure

After the opt-in, the lead magnet still has work to do. A strong delivery sequence increases consumption (which drives the "if free is this good..." reaction) and warms the subscriber for the desired action.

---

## File Output Structure

When finalizing, write all artifacts into `lead-magnet-output/`:

```
lead-magnet-output/
├── lead-magnet-content.md      # The actual downloadable resource
├── landing-page-copy.md        # The opt-in page copy
└── delivery-emails.md          # (optional) 3-email delivery sequence
```

---

## Pre-Delivery Checklist

```
## Pre-Delivery Checklist

### Lead Magnet Content
- [ ] Solves ONE specific problem (not three vague ones)
- [ ] Under 3 pages / consumable in under 10 minutes
- [ ] Every item starts with an action verb
- [ ] Includes a quick win in the first 3 items
- [ ] "What to Do Next" section points to the desired action
- [ ] No filler paragraphs or throat-clearing introductions
- [ ] Would be useful even without the paid offer

### Landing Page Copy
- [ ] Headline names a specific benefit, not a vague promise
- [ ] Subheadline adds specificity (number, timeframe, or audience)
- [ ] 3-5 benefit bullets follow the "feature so you can benefit" format
- [ ] CTA button text describes the action (not "Submit" or "Sign Up")
- [ ] Objection handler addresses the #1 hesitation
- [ ] Social proof section is included (even as placeholder)
- [ ] Repeat CTA appears below social proof
- [ ] No hype words: "revolutionary," "game-changing," "secret"
- [ ] Tone matches the strategy brief
```

## Delivery Email Sequence (3 emails)

Optional but recommended. If the user already has an email platform, offer to draft these.

### Email 1 — Immediate Delivery (sent within 60 seconds of opt-in)

**Goal:** deliver the resource, set expectations, reduce spam-folder risk.

```
Subject: Your [Resource Name] is here

Hey [First Name],

Here it is — as promised:

[Download Link]

Open it now, skim the first page, and try the first item. It takes
under 5 minutes and you will see the difference the same day.

One quick heads-up: over the next few days, I will send two more
emails with the thinking behind the [resource] and how to get the
most out of it. After that, you will hear from me about once a week.

If the [resource] is useful, reply and tell me which item you tried
first. I read every reply.

— [Sender Name]

P.S. If the download link does not work, just reply to this email
and I will send it directly.
```

### Email 2 — Context & Quick Win (24-48 hours later)

**Goal:** drive consumption, surface a quick win, establish voice.

```
Subject: Did the first [item] work?

Hey [First Name],

Yesterday you grabbed the [Resource Name]. If you tried the first
[item], you probably noticed [specific expected outcome].

Here is the one I would skip to next if you only have 10 minutes:

[Name of the single highest-impact item, with 2-3 sentences on why]

The reason it works: [one-sentence mechanism].

Most people try this one once and then build it into their workflow
permanently. If you try it this week, reply and tell me what
happened — good, bad, or confusing.

— [Sender Name]
```

### Email 3 — Bridge to Desired Action (3-5 days later)

**Goal:** transition from free resource to the desired next action (newsletter join, call booking, product page).

```
Subject: The bigger picture behind [Resource Topic]

Hey [First Name],

The [Resource Name] solves one slice of [larger topic]. But the
reason most people still struggle with [topic] is not the [slice]
— it is [the bigger, harder problem].

That is what I work on with [audience] every [day/week]. Here is
what that looks like:

- [Bullet 1: benefit of the next step]
- [Bullet 2]
- [Bullet 3]

If that sounds useful, the next step is [desired action]:

[CTA Link]

Not ready? No pressure. Stay on the list — the weekly emails cover
[topic themes] and every one includes something you can use that day.

— [Sender Name]
```

---

## Sequence Rules

- Deliver the resource in Email 1 above the fold. Never make them hunt for the link.
- Each follow-up email must be useful on its own, not just a nudge toward the paid offer.
- Reply prompts ("reply and tell me...") in Emails 1 and 2 improve deliverability and build relationship.
- Email 3 is the only one that pitches. Do not pitch earlier — it breaks the trust established by the free resource.
- If the desired action is "book a call" or "buy a product," Email 3 is the first ask. Add a fourth email 7 days later with a soft re-ask if no response.

---

## Platform Notes (informational)

- **ConvertKit / MailerLite / Kit:** automation triggers on form submission; paste the three email bodies directly into a sequence.
- **Substack / Ghost:** only Email 1 is natively supported (welcome email); use a separate tool for Emails 2-3 or skip the sequence.
- **Mailchimp:** requires a "Customer Journey" for multi-email delivery on the free tier.

The skill does not configure these platforms — it delivers the copy. Setup is the user's job.
