# Landing Page Copy — Full Reference

The landing page has one job: collect the email. Every element serves that one goal. Seven elements, in this order.

---

## 1. Headline (Benefit-Driven)

The headline must answer: "What will I get, and what will it do for me?"

### Formula Options

| Formula | Structure | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **The [Specific] [Type]** | The [adjective] [type] for [audience] | The 23-Point Content Repurposing Checklist for Solo Creators |
| **How to [Result] Without [Pain]** | How to [desired outcome] without [thing they hate] | How to Post on 5 Platforms Without Writing 5 Pieces of Content |
| **Stop [Pain]. Start [Gain].** | Stop [frustration]. Start [desired state]. | Stop Creating From Scratch. Start Repurposing Like a Pro. |
| **Get [Result] in [Timeframe]** | Get [specific outcome] in [believable time] | Get a Week of Content in 60 Minutes |
| **The [Number] [Things] [Audience] Use to [Result]** | Number + concrete items + audience + outcome | The 7 Scripts Top Freelancers Use to Close Clients in One Call |

**Default:** use "The [Specific] [Type]" formula. Most reliable, works for every lead magnet type.

### Headline Rules

- Include a specific number whenever possible (23-Point, 7 Scripts, 5-Day).
- Name the audience explicitly if the topic is narrow (solo creators, freelancers, SaaS founders).
- Avoid hype words: "revolutionary," "game-changing," "secret," "ultimate" (unless paired with a number).

---

## 2. Subheadline

One sentence that adds specificity or addresses the reader's situation directly. Layer the headline with either a number, a timeframe, or a concrete scenario.

**Examples:**

- "A step-by-step checklist that turns one piece of content into a full week of posts across Twitter, LinkedIn, Instagram, email, and video — in under 60 minutes."
- "A one-page reference guide with the exact steps, scripts, and timelines that cut onboarding from weeks to days — so new clients feel handled from day one."

---

## 3. Benefit Bullets (3-5)

Format every bullet as: **[Feature] so you can [benefit]** — lead with tangible thing, follow with outcome.

### Rules

- Start every bullet with a checkmark, arrow, or dash.
- Lead with the concrete deliverable, follow with the reader's outcome.
- At least one bullet addresses a specific fear or frustration.
- At least one bullet includes a number.

---

## 4. CTA Button Text

Describe what happens when they click. Never use form-mechanic language.

**Strong CTAs:**
- "Send Me the Checklist"
- "Get the Free Cheat Sheet"
- "Download the Template"
- "Send It to My Inbox"
- "Show Me the Scripts"

**Avoid:** "Submit," "Sign Up," "Subscribe," "Click Here." These describe the form, not the value — and consistently underperform in A/B tests.

---

## 5. Objection Handler

Place directly below the CTA. Address the #1 reason someone would hesitate.

| Objection | Handler |
|-----------|---------|
| "Is this actually free?" | "100% free. No credit card required. Just enter your email and it lands in your inbox in under 60 seconds." |
| "Will I get spammed?" | "You will receive the [resource] and occasional tips on [topic]. Unsubscribe with one click anytime." |
| "Is this just basic stuff I already know?" | "This is the exact [process/framework] used by [credibility proof]. Not theory — actionable steps you can use today." |
| "I do not have time for this" | "The entire [resource] takes under [X] minutes to read. Most people implement the first tip the same day." |
| "Will this actually work for me?" | "Written specifically for [narrow audience]. If you [specific situation], the first [item] applies to you on day one." |

**Default:** "Will I get spammed?" — the most universal objection for email opt-ins.

---

## 6. Social Proof

Include a section with clear placeholders the user can fill in later.

```
## Social Proof (fill in before publishing)

Option A — Testimonial:
"[Quote from someone who used the content/method]" — [Name], [Title/Context]

Option B — Number proof:
"Downloaded by [X]+ [audience type]" or "Join [X]+ [audience] who..."

Option C — Authority proof:
"Featured in [publication]" or "From the creator of [known thing]"
```

**Recommendation:** Option B for any user with an existing audience. "Join 200+ content creators" outperforms no social proof every time.

---

## 7. Repeat CTA

After social proof, repeat the CTA button and email field. Add one line of urgency or re-framing above it.

**Examples:**
- "Ready to stop creating from scratch? → [Send Me the Checklist]"
- "Stop losing clients in the first 30 days. → [Get the Free Cheat Sheet]"

Visitors who scroll past the hero need a second conversion point without scrolling back up.
