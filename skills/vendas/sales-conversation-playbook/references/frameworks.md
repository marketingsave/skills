# Frameworks Reference

Detailed breakdowns of SPIN, BANT, MEDDIC, and LAER for discovery, qualification, and objection handling.

## SPIN — Discovery Questioning

Use SPIN for pain exploration. Discovery questions outnumber pitch statements 3:1.

### Situation (3-8 min of 30-min call)
Understand the prospect's current state.
- "Tell me about your business — what do you do, who do you serve?"
- "How long have you been dealing with [problem area]?"
- "What have you tried so far?"
- "What's working? What isn't?"

### Problem (8-12 min)
Surface specific pain points.
- "What's the single biggest thing holding you back right now?"
- "How long has that been going on?"

### Implication (12-15 min)
Expand the cost of the problem.
- "What has this cost you — revenue, time, stress?"
- "If nothing changes in the next 6-12 months, what does that look like?"

### Need-Payoff (15-17 min)
Let the prospect articulate the value of solving.
- "If we could fix this in the next 90 days, what would that mean for you/your business?"
- "If we waved a magic wand, what's the ideal outcome?"

## BANT — Qualification (17-20 min)

Qualify before pitching. 30 minutes with someone who has no budget or authority is wasted.

- **Budget:** "Have you set aside a budget for this, or is that something we need to figure out?"
- **Authority:** "Is there anyone else involved in this decision?"
- **Need:** "On a scale of 1-10, how urgent is solving this?"
- **Timeline:** "If we agree this is a fit, when would you want to get started?"

## MEDDIC — Enterprise / Complex B2B Extension

For enterprise or complex B2B deals, extend BANT with MEDDIC:

- **Metrics:** What quantifiable outcome will success deliver? ("If this works, what KPI moves, and by how much?")
- **Economic buyer:** Who has final sign-off on the check? ("Who ultimately approves the spend?")
- **Decision criteria:** What must-haves and nice-to-haves will the buyer use to evaluate? ("What criteria are you comparing vendors on?")
- **Decision process:** What are the exact steps from today to signed contract? ("Walk me through how a decision like this gets made internally.")
- **Identified pain:** Is there explicit, acknowledged pain — not just curiosity? ("What happens if you do nothing?")
- **Champion:** Is there an internal advocate with power/influence who will sell for you when you're not in the room? ("Who else inside the company wants this to happen?")

## LAER — Objection Handling

Use LAER for every objection, no exceptions.

1. **Listen** — Let them finish. No interrupting.
2. **Acknowledge** — "That makes total sense." Empathy before rebuttal.
3. **Explore** — Ask what's really underneath the stated objection. Most objections are symptoms, not root causes.
4. **Respond** — Reframe + evidence + graceful exit if they stay firm.

### Principles
- Objections are requests for more information, not rejections.
- Empathy first, always. Never argue the objection.
- Acknowledge when an objection is valid — not every concern can be "handled."
- Respect "no." Two refusals = stop asking.
