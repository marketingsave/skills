# Objection Bank

Complete reference for LAER-based objection response cards, organized by category with frequency data.

## Category Priority

| Priority | Category | Examples | Frequency |
|----------|----------|----------|-----------|
| CRITICAL | Price | "Too expensive," "Can't afford it," "Cheaper elsewhere" | ~60% |
| HIGH | Timing | "Not right now," "Maybe next quarter," "Too busy" | ~20% |
| HIGH | Trust | "How do I know this works?" "Never heard of you" | ~10% |
| MEDIUM | Need | "I don't think I need this," "Already have a solution" | ~5% |
| MEDIUM | Authority | "Need to check with partner/boss" | ~5% |

## Response Card Template

For each top objection, build a card:

```
[Category] — "[Exact words]"
- What they're really saying: [underlying concern]
- Acknowledge: [empathetic opener]
- Explore: [clarifying question]
- Respond: [reframe + evidence]
- If they push back again: [graceful second response / exit]
```

## Pre-Built Response Cards

### PRICE — "It's too expensive"
- What they're really saying: "I'm not sure the outcome is worth $X right now."
- Acknowledge: "I totally get it — $X is a real investment, and you should be sure it's worth it."
- Explore: "When you think about the cost, is it that the amount feels high overall, or you're not sure you'll get the return?"
- Respond (ROI concern): "Let me share what [client] experienced — similar spot, invested $X, and [specific result] within [timeframe]. It paid for itself [multiplier]."
- Respond (budget concern): "We do have a payment plan — $Y/month — if that makes it manageable. I'd rather you join at a pace that feels comfortable."
- If they push back again: "No pressure at all. I'll send a recap. If the timing works later, the offer stands."

### TIMING — "I need to think about it"
- What they're really saying: "Something is holding me back but I can't or won't say it yet."
- Acknowledge: "Of course — this is an important decision."
- Explore: "When you say think about it — is there a specific concern I can address right now? Sometimes it helps to talk through the hesitation."
- Respond (vague): "Totally fine. I'll email a summary. Is there a good day this week to touch base, even just a quick text?"
- If they push back again: "Got it. I'll follow up in a few days. If it's a no, just let me know — no hard feelings."

### TIMING — "I'm not ready yet"
- Acknowledge: "Fair enough."
- Explore: "When do you see yourself being ready? What needs to happen between now and then?"
- Respond: Either book a follow-up on that date, or expose that "ready" is a moving target.

### TRUST — "I've been burned before" / "How do I know this works?"
- Acknowledge: "Completely valid — most [courses/agencies/products] in this space are terrible. I've seen it too."
- Explore: "What happened with the ones you tried? Content, format, something else?"
- Respond: "The #1 reason these fail is [root cause]. That's exactly what we solve with [specific mechanism]. If you get stuck, [accountability mechanism]."

### NEED — "We already have someone doing this"
- Acknowledge: "Great that you have support in place. I wouldn't want you to change something that's working."
- Explore: "How are results going? Are you hitting the growth targets you set at the start of the year?"
- Respond: "Sounds like [specific gap]. That's where we specialize — [proof point]. Would it help if I showed you what we did for a similar [company]?"

### AUTHORITY — "I need to talk to my partner/spouse/boss"
- Acknowledge: "Of course."
- Explore: "What do you think they'll want to know? Money, time commitment, or whether it'll work?"
- Respond: "Would it help if I put together a quick summary you can share? Or better — can we get the three of us on a 15-min call so I can answer their questions directly?"

### STALL — "Can you send me a proposal and I'll review it?"
- What they're really saying: "I want to end this conversation without saying no."
- Acknowledge: "Absolutely, I can put something together."
- Explore: "Before I do — so it's relevant — what are the 2-3 things that would need to be in the proposal for you to say yes?"
- Respond: "Perfect. I'll have it to you by [day]. Let's book 15 min on [day] to walk through it together — proposals are way more useful live."
