# SOW Legal Clauses — Detailed Guidance

This file expands the SOW sections that protect both parties: exclusions, acceptance criteria, change orders, sign-off, and late-payment terms.

## 5. EXCLUSIONS

The following are NOT included in this scope of work:
- [Specific task that might be assumed but is excluded]
- [Another exclusion]
- Ongoing maintenance, support, or training unless stated
- Any additional work outside the deliverables listed above

Additional work may be quoted separately upon request.

**Rules:**
- Think about what the client might assume is included. List it. This section is the shield.
- Omitting exclusions guarantees the client assumes everything is included.
- Common exclusions to consider: ongoing maintenance, copywriting, photography, hosting fees, third-party licensing, training, post-launch support, e-commerce features, translations, SEO beyond setup, analytics setup, email/domain configuration.

## 6. ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA

Each deliverable is considered accepted when:
- Client provides written approval (email confirmation is sufficient)
- If no response is received within [5] business days of delivery, the deliverable is deemed accepted
- Revisions requested after acceptance are billed at $[rate]/hour

**Rules:**
- Deemed-accepted clause (default 5 business days) is non-negotiable — it protects you from ghosting.
- Define what counts as "one round" of revision (consolidated feedback = one round).
- Cap revisions explicitly. Do NOT offer unlimited revisions.

## 7. FEES AND PAYMENT — Late-Payment Terms

- Total project fee: $[amount]
- Payment schedule:
  - [50%] upon signing: $[amount]
  - [25%] at [milestone]: $[amount]
  - [25%] upon final delivery: $[amount]
- Payment terms: Due within [X] days of invoice
- Late payment: [1.5%] per month on overdue balances

**Rules:**
- Never 100% on completion. Always an upfront deposit (default 50%).
- Late-payment fee specified (default 1.5%/month).
- Align milestones with payment schedule.

## 8. CHANGE ORDERS

Any changes to the scope, deliverables, or timeline require a written change order signed by both parties. Change orders may affect the project fee and timeline.

**Rules:**
- Mandatory. Without it, every scope change becomes a negotiation.
- Must be written, signed, and explicitly allow fee/timeline adjustments.
- Reference this clause whenever the client asks for mid-project additions.
