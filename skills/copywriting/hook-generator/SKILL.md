# Hook Generator

## When to Use This Skill

- Writing a YouTube/Reels/TikTok video and need a strong opening
- Crafting social media posts that stop the scroll
- Writing email subject lines or opening lines
- Starting a blog post or article with impact
- Creating ad copy that grabs attention in the first 2 seconds
- **Writing the first line of a social-media thread** (X/Twitter, LinkedIn, Threads) — see "Threads & Long-Form Social" section
- Testing multiple angles for the same piece of content

**DO NOT** use this skill for full threads, full posts, or article bodies. This is specifically for the hook — the opening line(s) that earn the read.

## Core Principle

A hook has three seconds to earn the next thirty. Every hook must create a gap between what the reader knows and what they want to know.

## Hook Formulas

### The 12 Proven Hook Types

| # | Type | Formula | Best For |
|---|------|---------|----------|
| 1 | Contrarian | "[Common belief] is wrong. Here's why." | Thought leadership, LinkedIn |
| 2 | Curiosity Gap | "There's a reason [surprising thing] and it's not what you think" | YouTube, blog posts |
| 3 | Specific Number | "[Number] [things] that [desirable outcome]" | Listicles, Instagram carousels |
| 4 | Story Open | "Last [month/Tuesday/year], I [specific event]..." | Email, long-form posts |
| 5 | Direct Challenge | "Stop [common mistake]. Do this instead." | Reels, TikTok, tweets |
| 6 | Bold Claim | "You can [impressive result] in [short timeframe]" | Sales pages, ads |
| 7 | Question | "What would you do if [relatable scenario]?" | Community posts, email |
| 8 | Before/After | "I went from [bad state] to [good state] by [method]" | Case studies, transformation content |
| 9 | Social Proof | "[Number] [people] already [did thing]. Here's what they learned." | Landing pages, emails |
| 10 | Confession | "I used to [embarrassing truth]. Then I discovered..." | Personal brand, newsletters |
| 11 | Urgency | "[Thing] is changing on [date]. Here's what you need to know." | Time-sensitive content |
| 12 | Pattern Interrupt | "[Unexpected statement that seems unrelated]" | Short-form video, tweets |

## Workflow

### Step 1: Understand the Content

Ask the user:
1. What's the topic or main point?
2. What format? (video, post, email, article, ad, **thread**)
3. What platform? (YouTube, Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok, X/Twitter, Threads, email, blog)
4. Who's the audience?
5. **For threads:** what's the most impressive or surprising element of the content? (drives the hook angle)

**Minimum needed: questions 1 and 2.** For threads, also get question 5.

### Step 2: Generate Hooks

Produce **5 hooks** (or **10 for threads**) using different formulas from the table above. For each hook:
- Label the hook type used
- Write the exact hook text
- Note the recommended platform fit

### Step 3: Rank and Recommend

Rank the hooks by predicted engagement and explain why the #1 pick works best for the specified format and audience.

## Threads & Long-Form Social

When the format is a **social media thread** (X/Twitter, LinkedIn, Threads), use these specialized rules in addition to the 12 formulas above.

### Why threads need special treatment

The hook IS the ad for the thread. If line 1 fails, lines 2–25 never get read. Threads also have platform-specific UI quirks (truncation, "show this thread" button, "see more" cutoff) that change which hooks work.

### Thread-Optimized Formulas

These 10 thread hook formulas are tuned for the "earn-the-read" job:

```
1. The Bold Claim
"[Impressive result]. Here's exactly how:"
Ex: "I grew my email list from 0 to 10,000 in 90 days. Here's exactly how (thread):"

2. The Curiosity Gap
"[Hint at something surprising] that nobody talks about."
Ex: "There's a pricing strategy that 10x'd my revenue. Nobody teaches it."

3. The Mistake Confession
"I [made a mistake] for [time period]. [What it cost me]."
Ex: "I undercharged for 3 years straight. It cost me $200K in lost revenue."

4. The Contrarian Take
"Unpopular opinion: [common practice] is [negative outcome]."
Ex: "Unpopular opinion: posting every day is killing your engagement."

5. The Direct Address
"If you're a [specific person] who [specific struggle], read this:"
Ex: "If you're a freelancer who hates pricing conversations, read this:"

6. The Result First
"[End result]. [Brief context]. A thread on what changed everything:"
Ex: "$0 to $50K/month in 18 months. No funding. No team. A thread on what changed everything:"

7. The Time-Bound Story
"I [did something] for [time period]. Here's what happened."
Ex: "I cold-emailed 500 prospects in 30 days. Here's what happened."

8. The List Tease
"[Number] [things] that [result]. Most people only know #1."
Ex: "7 pricing strategies that doubled my revenue. Most people only know #1."

9. The Question Hook
"Why do [successful people] always [do this specific thing]?"
Ex: "Why do the top 1% of freelancers never charge by the hour?"

10. The Before/After
"[Time] ago I was [bad state]. Today I [good state]. Here's the turning point:"
Ex: "12 months ago I was charging $50/hr. Today my minimum project is $10K. Here's the turning point:"
```

### Platform-Specific Adjustments for Threads

**X/Twitter**
- Hard limit: 280 characters.
- Hook must work WITH the "Show this thread" button.
- Ending with "(thread):" or "A thread:" signals there is more.
- First tweet IS the hook — nothing above it.

**LinkedIn**
- First 2 lines show before "see more" — these ARE the hook.
- Up to 3,000 chars total, but the hook is the first 2 lines.
- Professional tone — clickbait feels out of place.
- Line breaks after the hook create visual pause.

**Threads**
- Similar to X but allows more length.
- Can use formatting and line breaks.
- More casual than LinkedIn, more authentic than X.

### Thread Hook Scoring

Rate every thread hook on:

| Criteria | What It Measures |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Stop power** | Would this make someone pause mid-scroll? |
| **Curiosity** | Does it create an itch to know more? |
| **Specificity** | Does it include numbers, names, or concrete details? |
| **Platform fit** | Optimized for the target platform? |

### Thread Hook Checklist

```
- [ ] Under character limit for the platform
- [ ] Creates curiosity or tension in the first line
- [ ] Includes a specific number, result, or detail
- [ ] Does not give away the full answer (earns the read)
- [ ] Sounds natural, not forced or gimmicky
- [ ] Matches the actual content (no misleading hooks)
```

## Examples

### Example 1: YouTube Video About Email Marketing

**Topic:** Why most small businesses waste money on social media when email marketing has a higher ROI
**Format:** YouTube video (first 5 seconds spoken to camera)

**Generated hooks:**

1. **Contrarian:** "Social media marketing is a waste of money for most small businesses. I know that sounds insane, but look at these numbers." *(Best for: YouTube, LinkedIn)*
2. **Specific Number:** "I made $47,000 from one email list of 2,000 people. Here's the exact strategy." *(Best for: YouTube, Instagram Reels)*
3. **Story Open:** "Last March, I deleted all my social media apps for 30 days. My revenue went up. Here's what happened." *(Best for: YouTube, podcasts)*
4. **Direct Challenge:** "Stop posting on Instagram. Seriously. Your time is worth more than 47 likes." *(Best for: TikTok, Reels, tweets)*
5. **Bold Claim:** "You can build a $10K/month business with 500 email subscribers and zero social media followers." *(Best for: YouTube, ads)*

**Recommended:** Hook #3 (Story Open) — creates immediate curiosity, feels authentic, sets up the entire video.

### Example 2: Instagram Carousel About Pricing

**Topic:** How to raise your freelance rates without losing clients

1. **Contrarian:** "Charging less doesn't get you more clients. It gets you worse ones."
2. **Specific Number:** "5 scripts to raise your rates by 30% (without a single client leaving)"
3. **Confession:** "I charged $25/hour for 2 years. Then one email changed everything."
4. **Question:** "Would your best client leave you over a 20% price increase? (Spoiler: no)"
5. **Before/After:** "$25/hour → $150/hour in 8 months. Here's what I changed (it wasn't my skills)."

**Recommended:** Hook #5 — specific numbers + curiosity gap (the parenthetical) + aspirational transformation.

### Example 3: Cold Email Subject Line

**Topic:** Reaching e-commerce brands offering email marketing services
**Format:** Email subject line (under 40 chars)

1. **Curiosity Gap:** "your welcome email is losing you $2K/mo"
2. **Question:** "quick question about [Brand Name]'s emails"
3. **Specific Number:** "3 emails = $12K (for brands like yours)"
4. **Social Proof:** "did this for [Similar Brand] — thought of you"
5. **Pattern Interrupt:** "don't hire an email marketer"

**Recommended:** Hook #2 — personalized subject lines with brand name get 26% higher open rates; "quick question" implies low commitment.

### Example 4: X/Twitter Thread on Freelance Rates

**Topic:** How I doubled my freelance rate
**Format:** X thread (first tweet, ≤280 chars)

1. "I doubled my freelance rate in 90 days. Not a single client complained. Here's exactly what I changed:"
2. "The pricing conversation that changed my freelance career forever:"
3. "I was charging $75/hr for 2 years. Then a mentor told me something that made me feel sick:"
4. "If you're a freelancer charging less than $150/hr, you're making one of these 3 mistakes:"
5. "Unpopular opinion: raising your prices is the fastest way to get BETTER clients."
6. "Why the best freelancers never share their hourly rate (and what they do instead):"
7. "$75/hr → $150/hr in 90 days. Zero new skills. Zero new clients. Just one pricing shift."
8. "I asked 50 six-figure freelancers how they set their rates. Every single one said the same thing:"
9. "Stop charging by the hour. Here's the math on why it's costing you $50K/year:"
10. "3 months ago I raised my rate 100%. Here's what happened to my pipeline:"

**Recommended:** Hook #7 — concrete before/after numbers + "no new skills" subverts expectation + earns the read.

## Recovery & Fallbacks

- **User doesn't know their audience:** Generate hooks for a general audience, then ask which resonate — preference reveals the audience.
- **All hooks feel generic:** Ask for one specific detail, metric, or story. One real number transforms a generic hook into a compelling one.
- **User wants more than 5:** Generate a second round using the remaining unused formulas.
- **Hook doesn't fit the format:** Adapt — YouTube hooks are spoken (conversational), tweets need ≤280 chars, email subject lines ≤50 chars.
- **Topic is boring:** Find the most surprising or specific detail and lead with that.
- **No impressive results to cite:** Use curiosity gap or question formulas instead of result-first.
- **Hooks feel clickbaity:** Ground them in specificity. "I did something amazing" is clickbait. "I went from $50/hr to $5K projects" is specific and credible.

## Constraints

- **NEVER write clickbait the content can't deliver on.**
- Hooks must be truthful — no fabricated numbers or results.
- Match hook energy to format (casual for TikTok, professional for LinkedIn).
- Video hooks: ≤10 seconds when spoken aloud.
- Email subject lines: ≤50 characters, no ALL CAPS, no excessive punctuation.
- Thread hooks: respect platform char limits and UI cutoffs.
